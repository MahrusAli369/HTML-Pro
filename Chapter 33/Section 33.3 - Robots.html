Robots Meta Directive

The robots meta attribute controls whether search engine crawlers may index your page or follow its links. It’s recognized by major search engines and is essential for managing what content appears in search results. 

<meta name="robots" content="noindex">

This example instructs all search engines to not index the page. Other allowed directive values include:

No Value / Directive Meaning
1. all Equivalent to index, follow.
2. noindex Do not index the page.
3. nofollow Do not follow links on the page.
4. follow Links may be followed.
5. none Equivalent to noindex, nofollow.
6. noarchive No cached version in results.
7. nocache Synonym of noarchive (Bing).
8. nosnippet No snippet shown in results.
9. noodp Do not use Open Directory metadata.
10. notranslate No translation suggestions.
11. noimageindex Do not index images.
12. unavailable_after Hide after specific RFC-850 date.

Note: Search engines assume “allowed” by default. Only declare restrictions to avoid contradictions like index paired with noindex.
